Penne Arrabbiata

Bold, spicy Roman tomato sauce clinging to every ridge of MAIE penne — "arrabbiata" means angry, and this dish delivers heat.

Prep15 mins
Cook25 mins
Serves4
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • MAIE Penne Pasta400 g
  • Canned San Marzano tomatoes, crushed800 g (2 cans)
  • Garlic cloves, thinly sliced6 cloves
  • Red chilli flakes2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Extra virgin olive oil4 tbsp
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn1 handful
  • Sea salt & black pepperto taste
  • Parmesan cheese, grated (optional)for serving

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook MAIE penne until al dente — about 10–11 minutes. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until lightly golden, about 2 minutes. Watch carefully — burnt garlic ruins the dish.
  3. Add chilli flakes and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant. The oil should take on a reddish tint from the chilli.
  4. Pour in crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to low and cook uncovered for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and deepens in colour.
  5. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning. It should be bright, tangy, and noticeably spicy. Add more chilli if you prefer extra heat.
  6. Add drained penne to the sauce along with ¼ cup pasta water. Toss over medium heat for 2 minutes until the sauce coats every piece and clings to the ridges.
  7. Remove from heat, fold in fresh basil, and serve immediately in warm bowls. Top with grated Parmesan if desired.
Chef's tip: Penne rigate is ideal for arrabbiata because the ridges trap the spicy tomato sauce. For a smoother sauce, blend half the tomatoes before adding to the pan.
