Classic Mac & Cheese

Ultra-creamy, golden, and comforting — the ultimate mac & cheese made with MAIE macaroni.

Prep10 mins
Cook30 mins
Serves4–6
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • MAIE Macaroni450 g
  • Unsalted butter4 tbsp
  • All-purpose flour4 tbsp
  • Whole milk, warmed3 cups
  • Sharp cheddar cheese, grated2 cups
  • Gruyère cheese, grated1 cup
  • Dijon mustard1 tsp
  • Garlic powder½ tsp
  • Smoked paprika¼ tsp
  • Salt & white pepperto taste
  • Panko breadcrumbs (for topping)½ cup

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F). Butter a 9×13 inch baking dish. Cook MAIE macaroni in salted boiling water until just shy of al dente — about 7 minutes. Drain well and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, to make a blonde roux. The mixture should smell nutty but remain pale.
  3. Gradually pour in warm milk, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Cook 5–7 minutes until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  4. Remove from heat. Add cheddar and Gruyère in handfuls, whisking until completely melted and smooth. Stir in mustard, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Fold cooked macaroni into the cheese sauce until every piece is coated. Pour into the prepared baking dish and spread evenly.
  6. Mix panko with 1 tablespoon melted butter and sprinkle over the top. Bake 20–25 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling at the edges.
  7. Rest 5 minutes before serving — the sauce will set slightly and become even creamier. Garnish with extra paprika if desired.
Chef's tip: Using two cheeses creates depth — sharp cheddar for bold flavour, Gruyère for nutty meltiness. Grate cheese yourself; pre-shredded contains anti-caking agents that make sauce grainy.
