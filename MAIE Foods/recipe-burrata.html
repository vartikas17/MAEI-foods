Creamy Burrata Pasta

Silky cherry tomato sauce meets molten burrata — an indulgent dish ready in under 30 minutes.

Prep10 mins
Cook20 mins
Serves3–4
DifficultyMedium

Ingredients

  • MAIE Penne Pasta350 g
  • Fresh burrata cheese2 balls (250 g)
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved400 g
  • Heavy cream120 ml
  • Garlic cloves, minced3 cloves
  • Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
  • Fresh basil leaves1 cup
  • Red chilli flakes½ tsp
  • Salt & black pepperto taste

Method

  1. Cook MAIE penne in generously salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add cherry tomatoes cut-side down and cook without moving for 3 minutes until blistered and charred on one side.
  3. Flip tomatoes, add garlic and chilli flakes, and cook 2 more minutes until tomatoes begin to break down and release their juices.
  4. Pour in cream and simmer for 3 minutes, stirring gently. Season with salt and pepper. The sauce should be pink-orange and slightly thickened.
  5. Add drained penne and ¼ cup pasta water. Toss over medium heat for 2 minutes until the sauce coats every piece evenly.
  6. Remove from heat. Tear half the basil leaves and fold into the pasta. Divide among warm bowls.
  7. Place a whole burrata ball on each serving. Drizzle with olive oil, scatter remaining basil, and finish with cracked black pepper. Serve immediately while the burrata is soft and creamy.
Chef's tip: Add burrata at the very end — never cook it or the creamy centre will disappear. Room-temperature burrata yields the best molten texture when the pasta hits the bowl.
