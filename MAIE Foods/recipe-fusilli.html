Fusilli Primavera

A celebration of seasonal vegetables in a light, buttery sauce — colourful, fresh, and full of flavour.

Prep20 mins
Cook20 mins
Serves4
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • MAIE Farfalle or Penne400 g
  • Zucchini, diced1 medium
  • Red bell pepper, sliced1 large
  • Yellow bell pepper, sliced1 large
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved200 g
  • Carrot, julienned1 large
  • Garlic cloves, minced3 cloves
  • Olive oil & unsalted butter2 tbsp each
  • Vegetable stock½ cup
  • Parmesan, grated½ cup
  • Fresh basil & lemon zestfor finishing

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook MAIE pasta until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain.
  2. Heat olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add carrots and sauté 3 minutes — they take longest to soften.
  3. Add bell peppers and zucchini. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender-crisp with light colour on the edges.
  4. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant. Add cherry tomatoes and cook 2 minutes until they just begin to soften and release juice.
  5. Pour in vegetable stock and simmer 2 minutes to create a light sauce base. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Add cooked pasta and ¼ cup pasta water. Toss over high heat for 2 minutes until everything is well combined and the sauce lightly coats the pasta.
  7. Remove from heat. Fold in Parmesan, fresh basil, and lemon zest. Serve immediately in warm bowls with extra Parmesan on the side.
Chef's tip: "Primavera" means spring — use whatever fresh vegetables are in season. The key is cooking each vegetable just enough to keep vibrant colour and a slight crunch.
