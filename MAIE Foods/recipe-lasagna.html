Vegetable Lasagna

Layers of MAIE lasagna sheets, roasted vegetables, ricotta, and molten cheese — a showstopper for any table.

Prep40 mins
Cook50 mins
Serves8
DifficultyMedium

Ingredients

  • MAIE Lasagna sheets12 sheets
  • Ricotta cheese500 g
  • Fresh spinach, wilted & chopped300 g
  • Zucchini, sliced2 medium
  • Eggplant, sliced1 medium
  • Marinara sauce4 cups
  • Mozzarella, shredded3 cups
  • Parmesan, grated1 cup
  • Egg1 large
  • Olive oil, salt, nutmeg, pepperto taste

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Brush zucchini and eggplant slices with olive oil, season with salt, and roast on a baking sheet for 20 minutes until tender and lightly charred.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Par-boil MAIE lasagna sheets for 4 minutes until pliable but not fully cooked. Drain and lay flat on oiled baking sheets to prevent sticking.
  3. In a bowl, combine ricotta, wilted spinach, egg, ½ cup Parmesan, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  4. Spread 1 cup marinara in the bottom of a 9×13 inch baking dish. Layer: pasta sheets, ricotta mixture, roasted vegetables, mozzarella, and sauce. Repeat for 3 full layers.
  5. Finish with a final layer of pasta, remaining sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Cover tightly with foil.
  6. Bake covered for 30 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 15–20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling. The centre should register 75°C if using a thermometer.
  7. Rest at least 15 minutes before slicing — this allows the layers to set and makes clean cuts possible. Serve with fresh basil and extra Parmesan.
Chef's tip: Roasting vegetables first removes excess moisture, preventing a watery lasagna. Make ahead and refrigerate overnight — flavours deepen and it slices even better the next day.
