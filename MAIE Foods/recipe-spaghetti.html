Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

A Roman classic — garlic, olive oil, and chilli in perfect harmony over al dente MAIE pasta.

Prep10 mins
Cook15 mins
Serves4
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • MAIE Vermicelli or Spaghetti400 g
  • Extra virgin olive oil80 ml (⅓ cup)
  • Garlic cloves, thinly sliced8 cloves
  • Red chilli flakes (peperoncino)1 tsp
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped½ cup
  • Sea saltto taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepperto taste
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated (optional)for serving

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add 2 tablespoons of salt — the water should taste like the sea. This is the only chance to season the pasta itself.
  2. While the water heats, slice the garlic as thinly and evenly as possible. Uneven slices will cook at different rates and some may burn.
  3. Add MAIE pasta to the boiling water and cook 1 minute less than the package directions for al dente texture. Before draining, reserve 1½ cups of starchy pasta water.
  4. In a large skillet (big enough to hold all the pasta), warm olive oil over medium-low heat. Add garlic and cook slowly, stirring often, until golden and fragrant — about 3–4 minutes. Do not let it brown or turn bitter.
  5. Add chilli flakes to the oil and stir for 30 seconds until the kitchen smells incredible. If the garlic is done before the pasta, remove the pan from heat temporarily.
  6. Transfer drained pasta directly into the skillet. Add ½ cup pasta water and increase heat to medium. Toss vigorously with tongs for 2 minutes — the starch in the water emulsifies with the oil to create a silky coating.
  7. Continue adding pasta water, a splash at a time, until the sauce clings to every strand. The pasta should look glossy, not oily or dry.
  8. Remove from heat. Toss in parsley, season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately in warm bowls. Finish with grated Parmesan if desired.
Chef's tip: The secret to authentic aglio e olio is low, patient heat for the garlic and aggressive tossing at the end. Never rinse the pasta after draining — the starch is essential for the sauce.
